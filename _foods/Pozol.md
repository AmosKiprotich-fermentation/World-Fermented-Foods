## Description
Pozol is a traditional fermented maize beverage or dough consumed in southern Mexico, particularly in Chiapas and Tabasco.

## Fermentation process
- Maize kernels nixtamalized with lime (alkaline cooking)
- Ground into a dough (masa)
- Dough fermented spontaneously for 1–3 days
- Consumed diluted as a beverage or eaten as dough

## Reported microbiology (examples)
- LAB: *Lactobacillus* spp., *Streptococcus* spp.
- Yeasts: *Saccharomyces* spp. (reported in some preparations)

## Functional relevance
- Improved nutritional quality due to nixtamalization and fermentation
- Production of organic acids
- Important role in traditional diets and hydration
