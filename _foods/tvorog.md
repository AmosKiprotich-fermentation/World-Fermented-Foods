## Overview
Tvorog is a traditional fermented dairy product widely consumed in Eastern Europe. It is a fresh curd cheese produced through lactic acid fermentation of milk followed by separation of the curd from whey.

## Raw Materials
- Cow’s milk
- Starter culture or naturally fermented milk

## Fermentation Process
Milk is allowed to ferment at moderate temperatures until acidification causes casein proteins to coagulate. The resulting curd is gently heated and drained to separate whey, producing a soft curd cheese known as tvorog.

## Microbial Ecology
Fermentation is dominated by:
- *Lactococcus lactis*
- *Streptococcus thermophilus*

These lactic acid bacteria convert lactose into lactic acid, which induces coagulation and contributes to flavour development.

## Functional and Nutritional Aspects
- High protein content  
- Improved digestibility of lactose  
- Soft, mildly acidic flavour  

## Cultural Significance
Tvorog is a staple dairy product in Russia, Ukraine, and neighbouring countries and is commonly consumed with bread, fruit, or used in traditional pastries and desserts.
